Maneuverability

So far we've been only been looking at pitch. However there are two more axes on which a foil can rotate - roll and yaw. Generally when turning the board, this will involve some yaw and roll.

Roll and Yaw

Front Foil properties:

Wingspan

Foils with larger wingspans are harder to roll. This is one of the downsides of larger High Aspect Foils.

Anhedral

Anhedral is the down turning of the two wings on the foil:

Anhedral will reduce roll stability, increasing roll maneuverability.

Beginners will want more stable foils in roll. They'll be naturally using bigger foils that are more stable. Foils that are very wide are actually more difficult to handle than medium wingspan beginner foils

More advanced foilers will desire a turn-ier foil, especially for waves.

Winglets

Some front foils have up or downturned tips. These have two effects:

  • Reduce maneuverability
  • Reduce Induced drag (reduces the vortices)

They have gone out of favour, as they have a disadvantage of throwing the rider off if the wingtips breach.

Stabiliser properties:

The type of stabiliser used will also effect the maneuverability of the setup. The following properties will have an effect:

Upturned tips

Some stabilisers are flat, and some have upturned tips

These upturned tips resist yaw, making the setup more directional.

Dihedral

Dihedral is the angle the two wings of the stabiliser make to each other.

Dihedral reduces the maneuverability of the setup by increasing the roll stability - if you try to roll it will provide some resistance.

Fuselage length

By reducing fuselage length, maneuverability is increased in yaw. This is because it reduces the leverage required to yaw the foil.

Summary

We can increase maneuverability by having:

  • Smaller wingspans
  • Anhedral
  • Shorter fuselages
  • Flat stabilisers
