Getting Foiling

The first thing we need to learn when wingfoiling, is how to get up onto the foil

From the Foil Knowledge section, we know that lift is determined as

  1. Size of the foil
  2. Speed of the foil through the water
  3. Lift Coefficient - (related to the Angle of Attack of the foil)

To lift the rider out of the water requires sufficient speed, lift coefficient and foil size.

Beginners

Beginners will find it hard to get up on the foil. They will not have the ability yet to artificially increase speed and lift coefficient through pumping.

They can however use a big foil (1700-2500cm2) to help them, and go out in plenty of wind (15kts+) to push them along at a sufficient speed.

Intermediate/Advanced

With better balance, riders are able to pump the board, to get it foiling in lighter winds

This increases speed
