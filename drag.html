Drag

Foils produce drag when going through the water. There are two types of drag that affect foils:

  1. Parasitic drag - this is the drag caused by the resistance of the water. It increases as the square of the speed.
  2. Induced drag - this is the drag cause by creating lift, which induces vortices. This is bigger at high Angles of Attack (slow speeds)

Total drag is the combination of the two:

From the graph we can see that there is a moderate speed at which drag is at a minimum. We call this Minimum-Drag speed. This speed is the most efficient and requires the least amount of power from the sail/wing or wave. If you are doing a low power activity like pump-foiling, you will want to do it at this speed.

Stable and unstable speed

We call the plot of total drag against speed the "drag curve"

Because of the shape of the drag curve, we can break it down into two regions:

  1. SPEED UNSTABLE - here, any decrease in speed increases drag. With no increase in power, this results in a further loss of speed, leading to a stall
  2. SPEED STABLE - here, a decrease in speed reduces drag, so less power is needed to maintain the new speed.

We always want to be riding in the speed stable region.
