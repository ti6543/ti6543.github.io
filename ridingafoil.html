The effect of speed on the balance point

We've just seen that the foil will have a lower Angle of Attack at increased speed.

However, because the stabiliser is upside down, any decrease in Angle of Attack on the front wing INCREASES Angle of Attack on the stabiliser.

This increases the downward lift, which shifts the balance point forward. As a consequence the rider needs to have their weight further foward at higher speeds.

The foil setup (fuselage length, stabiliser size, shim) will affect how far forward the rider will need to move their weight at higher speeds. More on this later.

Drag pitch down effect:

At higher speeds, the drag of the foil adds a nose down, the opposite effect to the stabiliser.

Generally the nose up effect of the stabiliser is larger than the nose down effect of drag, but this is not always the case

Some setups can become very front footed at high speed, some can stay almost neutral. Neutral setups are great for wave riding.

Putting it altogether

To control the foil, the rider needs to control both:

  1. The upward lift. This is done by changing the angle of attack on the front foil
  2. The rotational forces. This is done by adjusting weight forward and aft

Fortunately, both of these require moving the rider weight forward at higher speeds, and back at lower speeds.
