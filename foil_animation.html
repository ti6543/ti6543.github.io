Foil balance simulator

This allows you to look at the effects of changing the setup of your foil

How to use:

  • Adjust the setup on the right of the screen
  • You can change the speed at the bottom. It is limited by the setup
  • The circle at the bottom shows where the balance point of the foil is - this is where the rider needs to stand
  • The line underneath shows the limits of balance point at low and high speed.
  • A long line underneath means a very stable setup. Good for beginners going at a constant speed on flat water. The downside is that at faster speeds, the rider weight needs to be very far forward
  • A short line underneath is better for wave riding, where speeds vary a lot. You do need to be more accurate with your weight, so better for advanced riders

Notes:

  • The upper speed limit is defined as AoA = 0. The foil could go faster, but is very difficult to control as tiny changes in pitch result in large lift changes
  • The lower speed limit is defined as AoA = 15 (stall AoA).
  • The front foil is assumed to have the same mounting point, regardless of size. Some brands (e.g. Armstrong) are fixed at the rear of the front foil, meaning a bigger foil extends forwards, resulting in effective a longer fuselage
  • I have assumed a foil where AoA at -5degrees creates zero lift, for both front wing and stabiliser
  • Speed is also limited by drag, which I haven't modelled for simplicity
  • I have assumed a -2 angle of incidence on the stabiliser
  • The speeds are relative only, and not meant to be an accurate model
