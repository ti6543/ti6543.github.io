Foil setup (Advanced)

On the foil setup page, we saw how to set up the board and foil so that it is balanced for cruising speed, giving a comfortable ride. We can however change various aspects of the setup to change how a foil rides

We're not going to look at the effect of changing the front foil yet, but will in later pages

Pitch stability

Pitch stability is three things:

  • How much of a bodyweight shift is required to pitch up or down
  • How quickly the board will pitch up or down if bodyweight is in the wrong position
  • How much the foil setup will try to return to where it was after hitting turbulence

Beginners will want good pitch stability, as they won't be as accurate and quick in shifting their bodyweight.

More advanced riders will want to be able to adjust their pitch more easily, to cope better with a rider range of speeds

You can increase stability by using:

  • Longer fuselages
  • Bigger stabilisers
  • Negative shim

Alternatively, to loosen up the pitch stability you can do the opposite, using shorter fuselages and smaller stabilisers

Reducing drag

For maximum efficiency, we want to reduce drag. This is best done by reducing stabiliser size.

Stabilisers with negative shim also produce more drag, so these can be eliminated

By doing this, you're going to reduce NOSE UP pressure, so will have to rectify it with either:

  • Moving the track forward in the box
  • Using a longer fuselage (this can add a tiny amount of drag too)

Balance point vs speed

From earlier, we learnt that the balance point shifts at low and high speeds

Stabiliser

As speed increases, the stabiliser produces more downward lift, lifting the nose UP. This is both due to extra speed (speed squared), and extra Angle of Attack

Drag

Drag increases due to extra speed, pushing the nose DOWN. The effect is purely proportional to the square of speed. Because of this, it won't be as big an effect as the stabiliser

Centre of Pressure change

At low speed the centre of pressure can move forward

The movement is small, maybe only 1-2cm. However, because the rider may only be standing a few centimetres ahead of the CofP of the front wing, the relative change can be large.

The profile of the foil changes how far the CofP moves, and at what speed
