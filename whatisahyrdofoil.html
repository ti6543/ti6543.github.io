What is a hydrofoil?

A hydrofoil is an object that produces a lift force when passing through water:

A consequence of producing lift is that it also induces drag (more on this later)

How much lift it creates is dependent on three things:

  1. Size and shape of the foil
  2. Speed of the foil through the water
  3. Angle of Attack of the foil

1. Size and shape

A bigger and fatter foil will provide more lift than a smaller thinner one.

2. Speed

A hydrofoil produces more lift at faster speeds. Lift is proportional to the square of the speed, so going twice as fast results in four times as much lift

3. Angle of Attack

The angle of attack is the angle between the foil and the incoming water flow:

As Angle of Attack increases, more lift is produced. However, there is a limit to this. At around 15 degrees the foil can no longer produce more lift with increasing Angle of Attack, and it starts decreasing. This is know as a stall.

Controlling the foil

We always need the foil to be producing the correct amount of lift, at a range of speeds. Too little and we sink back down to the water, whilst too much results in us flying up into the air (breaching).

The rider will control the amount of lift by varying the Angle of Attack. At slow speeds a high Angle of Attack is required, and at high speeds a low Angle of Attack

Slow - high Angle of Attack

Fast - low Angle of Attack
