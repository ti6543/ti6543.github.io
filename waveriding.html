Wave riding

Prerequisite - once comfortable gliding downwind with no power

Conditions - Knee to Waist high swell and Wind - gusts of 14kts to 30kts

Wingfoiling in swell and waves is a lot of fun, and what most people aspire to.

The foil makes conditions that would be no good for windsurf-wavesailing or surfing into excellent days

Common Errors - Lack of speed

The big mistake people make when learning to flat the wing on swell is to start off too slowly

Again, remember the drag curve:

So, if we enter slowly, we rapidly end up in the SPEED UNSTABLE region, and this leads to a rapid loss of speed, followed by a stall

Instead, if we enter above Minimum-Drag speed our drag is lower, and it is easier to regain any lost speed (e.g. in a flat section of the wave)

How to flag

The first thing to learn is flagging the wing.

You already have some experience of this from gybing, where you learnt to glide downwind with no power in the wing

  1. Ride along across or slightly downwind. Keep good speed
  2. As you spot a line of swell, bear off onto it
  3. Once comfortable, let go with the backhand, and move it to the flagging handle of the wing
  4. Now, let go with the old front-hand. Keep your weight forward
  5. Glide along on the wave, going downwind with it
  6. The wing will flag out behind us
